# Required Documents by Shipment Type

This reference lists the documents MakroShip requires for each type of international
shipment. Missing or incomplete documents are the leading cause of a CUS-HOLD status.
(Sample data — for testing.)

## Documents explained

- **Commercial Invoice.** States what is being shipped, the quantity, the value, and the
  buyer and seller. Customs uses it to calculate duty and VAT. It must show a realistic
  value; under-declaring leads to inspection or penalties.
- **Packing List.** Lists how the goods are physically packed (number of boxes, weight,
  dimensions). It helps customs match the declaration to the actual parcel.
- **Certificate of Origin.** States the country where the goods were produced. It can lower
  or raise the duty rate, depending on trade agreements.
- **Movement Certificate (A.TR / EUR.1).** Used for trade between countries with a customs
  union or free-trade agreement to claim a reduced or zero duty rate. The A.TR is commonly
  used for goods moving between Türkiye and the EU.
- **MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheet).** Required for shipments that contain chemicals,
  batteries, or other regulated substances. It describes the hazards and handling rules.
- **Dangerous Goods Declaration.** A signed statement classifying hazardous contents and
  confirming they are packed to the applicable transport regulations.
- **Insurance Certificate.** Proof that the shipment is insured for its declared value.
  Usually requested for high-value goods.
- **Import License / Permit.** Some product categories (for example medicines, certain
  electronics, or food) require a permit from a destination-country authority before they
  can be imported.

## Requirements by shipment type

### Commercial (business-to-business) shipment
Required: Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Certificate of Origin. Strongly recommended for
EU lanes: A.TR or EUR.1 movement certificate to claim preferential duty. If the value is
high, add an Insurance Certificate.

### Personal or gift shipment
Required: a simple Proforma Invoice describing the contents and stating a value, even for a
gift. Gifts are not automatically duty-free; most countries apply a low de-minimis threshold
above which duty and VAT still apply.

### Sample shipment (no commercial value)
Required: Commercial Invoice marked "Sample — no commercial value" with a nominal value for
customs. A zero value is not accepted; customs still needs a figure to assess.

### Dangerous goods shipment
Required: Commercial Invoice, Packing List, MSDS, and a signed Dangerous Goods Declaration.
The parcel must also carry the correct hazard labels. Missing DG paperwork stops the
shipment immediately and can incur fines.

### Regulated goods (medicine, food, some electronics)
Required: the standard commercial set plus the relevant Import License or Permit. Without the
permit the shipment is held at CUS-HOLD until the importer supplies it, or it is returned.

## Common mistakes that cause a hold

The most frequent reasons a shipment lands in CUS-HOLD are: a missing commercial invoice, a
declared value that looks too low for the goods, a vague goods description such as "gift" or
"parts" with no detail, and a missing permit for a regulated product. Providing a clear,
itemised invoice with a realistic value prevents most holds.
