# Incoterms 2020 — Plain-Language Summary

Incoterms are standard three-letter trade terms that define, for an international sale, who
arranges and pays for transport, who carries the risk at each point, and who handles export
and import formalities. This is a simplified summary in plain language for shipment-support
purposes. (Sample reference — for testing.)

## Why the Incoterm matters for a shipment

The Incoterm agreed between buyer and seller decides two things that show up constantly in
support questions: who pays import duty and VAT, and at what point the risk of loss or damage
passes from seller to buyer. For example, under DDP the seller covers duties; under every
other term the buyer normally does.

## Terms for any mode of transport

- **EXW — Ex Works.** The seller just makes the goods available at their own premises. The
  buyer arranges and pays for everything after that, including export clearance. Maximum
  responsibility sits with the buyer.
- **FCA — Free Carrier.** The seller hands the goods, cleared for export, to a carrier chosen
  by the buyer. Risk passes at hand-over.
- **CPT — Carriage Paid To.** The seller pays for carriage to a named destination, but risk
  passes to the buyer once the goods are given to the first carrier — not at the destination.
- **CIP — Carriage and Insurance Paid To.** Same as CPT, but the seller also buys transport
  insurance for the buyer.
- **DAP — Delivered at Place.** The seller delivers to a named place in the destination
  country, ready for unloading. Import duty and unloading are the buyer's responsibility.
- **DPU — Delivered at Place Unloaded.** Like DAP, but the seller also unloads the goods at
  the destination. This is the only term where the seller unloads.
- **DDP — Delivered Duty Paid.** The seller delivers to the destination and pays everything,
  including import duty and VAT. Maximum responsibility sits with the seller. This is the term
  where the receiver does not pay customs charges.

## Terms for sea and inland waterway only

- **FAS — Free Alongside Ship.** The seller delivers the goods alongside the vessel at the
  port of shipment.
- **FOB — Free on Board.** The seller delivers the goods on board the vessel; risk passes
  once the goods are on board.
- **CFR — Cost and Freight.** The seller pays the cost and freight to the destination port,
  but risk passes when the goods are on board at origin.
- **CIF — Cost, Insurance and Freight.** Like CFR, but the seller also buys marine insurance
  for the buyer.

## How to read them quickly

The letters roughly signal who does more: terms starting with E or F put most of the work on
the buyer, terms starting with C split it (seller pays freight, buyer carries risk in
transit), and terms starting with D put most of the work on the seller. For customs questions,
the key split is simple: DDP means the seller pays import charges; all other terms mean the
buyer (receiver) pays.
